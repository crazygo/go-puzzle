# Lighting Score Rubric

Score each dimension from 0.0 to 1.0, then multiply by its weight. The total score is the weighted sum after caps.

## Dimension Weights

| Dimension | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Exposure / tonemapping | 0.25 |
| Highlight falloff / sheen | 0.25 |
| Ambient / fill separation | 0.15 |
| Contact / cast shadows | 0.15 |
| Stone light response | 0.10 |
| Grid readability under lighting | 0.10 |

## 1. Exposure / Tonemapping, 0.25

Assess board top-surface luminance relative to golden.

Score high when:
- Board top exposure is close to golden.
- Bright regions preserve grid visibility.
- No large highlight region clips toward white.
- Tonemapping preserves midtone gradation.

Score low when:
- Board top is globally overexposed or underexposed.
- Bright areas wash out grid lines.
- Tonemapping compresses midtones into a flat bright plane.

Use wording:
- "Top surface exposure is higher/lower than golden."
- "Bright-region tonemapping compresses grid contrast."
- "Midtone gradation is weaker/stronger than golden."

## 2. Highlight Falloff / Sheen, 0.25

Assess specular/sheen highlight shape, softness, direction, and falloff.

Score high when:
- Highlight is broad, soft, and spatially close to golden.
- No localized band dominates the board.
- Bright transition length is similar to golden.

Score low when:
- Sheen creates a localized stripe or patch.
- Highlight is too hard, too saturated, or clipped.
- Key/sheen direction creates an unintended visual band across the grid.

Use wording:
- "Key/sheen contribution creates a localized band instead of broad falloff."
- "Highlight transition is shorter/longer than golden."
- "Specular/sheens are over-concentrated in the upper-right board region."

## 3. Ambient / Fill Separation, 0.15

Assess shadow-side value separation and fill strength.

Score high when:
- Lit and shadowed sides separate similarly to golden.
- Fill light supports visibility without flattening depth.
- Side edge and stone back sides retain readable shadow values.

Score low when:
- Ambient/fill lifts shadows too much.
- Shadow regions are too dark and lose information.
- Board and stones lose light-side/shadow-side structure.

Use wording:
- "Ambient/fill lifts shadow values too much; shadow-side separation is weaker."
- "Fill is insufficient; shadow-side values fall below golden."

## 4. Contact / Cast Shadows, 0.15

Assess shadows generated by stones and board edge.

Score high when:
- Stones have stable contact shadow/AO.
- Cast shadow softness and direction are close to golden.
- Stones visually sit on the board.

Score low when:
- Stones appear detached due to weak contact shadow.
- Cast shadows are too hard, too soft, too long, or too short.
- Shadow direction conflicts with apparent key light.

Use wording:
- "Contact shadow under stones is weaker than golden."
- "Cast shadow edge is harder/softer than golden."
- "AO radius under stones is smaller/larger than golden."

## 5. Stone Light Response, 0.10

Assess how stones respond to current lighting, not stone geometry or game state.

Score high when:
- Black-stone highlight size/intensity resembles golden.
- White-stone lit and shadow sides remain readable.
- Stone response is consistent with board lighting direction.

Score low when:
- Black-stone specular lobe is too small/bright or too large/dull.
- White stones lose separation from the board in bright areas.
- Stone highlights conflict with board highlight direction.

Use wording:
- "Black-stone specular lobe is smaller/brighter than golden."
- "White-stone midtone separation from board is weaker."

## 6. Grid Readability Under Lighting, 0.10

Assess lighting impact on grid line readability.

Score high when:
- Grid contrast remains readable in both bright and shadow areas.
- Lighting modulation feels consistent across the board.
- Grid does not overpower the board.

Score low when:
- Grid lines disappear inside highlights.
- Grid contrast varies too much across lit and shadowed areas.
- Grid becomes visually dominant due to excessive contrast.

Use wording:
- "Grid contrast falls below golden in high-exposure regions."
- "Grid readability is inconsistent across lit and shadowed areas."

## Caps

Apply caps after weighted score:

- If debug panel covers the board region, cap total score at `0.60`.
- If RGB axes or debug guides are visible in the board region, cap total score at `0.79`.
- If candidate and golden do not show comparable board regions, cap total score at `0.70`.
- If candidate sidecar is missing entirely, cap total score at `0.85`.
- If sidecar exists but `commit_id` is missing, do not cap by default; report metadata incomplete.

## Iteration Target Selection

Select the next single target from the lowest scoring primary cause:

- Low exposure score with global brightness issue: `tone_mapping_exposure`.
- Low highlight score with localized stripe/patch: `sheen_specular_contribution`.
- Low ambient/fill score: `ambient_fill_balance`.
- Low contact/cast score: `contact_shadow_ao`.
- Low stone response score caused by highlight response: `stone_light_response`.
- Low grid score caused by lighting washout: `grid_lighting_readability`.
- If key direction/intensity is clearly primary rather than sheen: `key_light_intensity_or_direction`.

If two dimensions tie, choose the one earlier in this priority list:
1. `sheen_specular_contribution`
2. `tone_mapping_exposure`
3. `key_light_intensity_or_direction`
4. `ambient_fill_balance`
5. `contact_shadow_ao`
6. `grid_lighting_readability`
7. `stone_light_response`
